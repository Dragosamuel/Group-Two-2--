2. Gathering Requirements

To build the right system, we first need to understand everyone's needs. We used two simple methods:

üó£Ô∏è

Interviews

We talked directly to the stakeholders to ask them what they need and what problems they face.

üßê

Observation

We watched the staff and customers to see how the restaurant currently operates and identify hidden challenges.

What We Learned from Stakeholders

  • The Owner said: "I need to see real-time sales reports and I want to offer online ordering to get more customers."
  • The Manager said: "Managing table reservations and staff schedules is a manual, chaotic process. I need a system to automate this."
  • A Waiter said: "Handwritten orders sometimes lead to mistakes. Splitting bills for large groups is also very difficult and slow."
  • We Observed: The Chef struggles with paper-based inventory, and customers sometimes wait a long time to be seated or to pay.

3. Documenting Requirements: The Blueprint

After gathering information, we write it down in a structured way. This document acts as the blueprint for our new system.

  • Functional Requirements: What the system must do.
    • Example: Process customer payments.
  • Non-Functional Requirements: How the system must be.
    • Example: The system must be easy to use.
  • User Requirements: A user's goal, written from their perspective.
    • Example: "As a customer, I want to order food online."

Examples of Dasah's Requirements

Functional (The "What")

  • Point of Sale (POS): To take orders and payments.
  • Order Management: To send orders to the kitchen.
  • Reservation Management: To handle table bookings.
  • Inventory Management: To track food stock.
  • Online Ordering: To allow customers to order via a website.

Non-Functional (The "How")

  • User-Friendly: Must be easy for staff to learn.
  • Reliable: Must not crash during busy hours.
  • Secure: Must protect customer payment data.

5. Prioritizing: What's Most Important?

We can't build everything at once. We use the MoSCoW method to decide what to build first.

M - Must Have

Critical for the system to work at all. The project will fail without these.

S - Should Have

Important and add a lot of value, but not absolutely critical for launch.

C - Could Have

Desirable "nice-to-have" features if time and resources permit.

W - Won't Have

Features that are out of scope for now but might be considered in the future.

Dasah's Priorities using MoSCoW

Must Have

  • Point of Sale (POS) System
  • Order Management
  • System Security

Should Have

  • Reservation Management
  • Inventory Management
  • Sales Reporting
  • Online Ordering

Could Have

  • Menu Management
  • Staff Scheduling

Won't Have (This Time)

  • Advanced Customer Loyalty Program

6. Requirement Traceability: Connecting the Dots

This is a way to track each requirement from its origin (who asked for it) to its completion. It ensures no requirement gets lost.

Summary & Next Steps

We have successfully planned the new Information System for Dasah Restaurant by following these key steps:

  1. Identified the stakeholders and their needs through interviews and observation.
  2. Documented the requirements in a clear and structured way.
  3. Validated the requirements with stakeholders to ensure accuracy.
  4. Prioritized what needs to be built first using the MoSCoW method.
  5. Established a plan for tracing requirements.
